Visualizing the System of Hebrew Accents

This page is designed to provide a visual way to understand the rules of Biblical Hebrew cantillation, a system of markings called accents that provide grammatical information (similar to English punctuation, but much more detailed). It is based on The Syntax of Masoretic Accents by James D. Price, except here Kadma V'Azla is treated as one accent instead of two. Note that it only applies to the books of the Tanakh (the Hebrew Bible) except Esther, Proverbs, and Job - which use a different system.

Rules: Within a row, you can only move directly left, although you may optionally skip any conjunctive accents (those in parentheses). Once you reach the end of the row, you may then jump to any cell in that column or a column to the right.

Colors: When hovering over the tables below:

  • Light red indicate all accents which could have come before the current accent (without jumping),
  • Orange indicates the current accent or one its substitutes,
  • Yellow indicates all accents which can come immediately after the current accent, and
  • Light green indicates all accents which could come further after the current accent (without jumping).

Note: Sometimes more than one conjunctive accent (one in parentheses) will appear in a row. Aside from the two cases accounted for in the substitution table below, this additional accent is almost always a Munach, although sometimes it is a Mapach, a Kadma, or a Darga (in decreasing order of frequency).

* = Can only be used once, as the first of its rank or higher before Sof-pasuk (the end of the verse).
